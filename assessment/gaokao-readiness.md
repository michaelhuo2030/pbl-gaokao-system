# 高考 Readiness: Ensuring Exam Competitiveness

## The Non-Negotiable Claim

This system must produce students who score **at or above** traditionally-educated peers on 高考.

If it cannot, it fails — regardless of how much agency, motivation, or creativity students demonstrate. The Chinese education market will not adopt a system that trades exam results for "soft skills."

## Why PBL Students Should Outperform (The Thesis)

### Deep Understanding > Surface Memorization

| Exam Challenge | Deep Understanding Advantage |
|---|---|
| Novel problem formats | PBL students have solved unfamiliar problems for years; they analyze rather than pattern-match |
| Multi-step reasoning | PBL projects routinely require chained reasoning; exam problems are simpler by comparison |
| Reading comprehension | PBL students have read extensively for real purposes; comprehension is natural, not drilled |
| Essay writing | PBL students have written hundreds of pages for real audiences; they have voice and arguments |
| 文言文 translation | Students who've used 文言文 for historical research have genuine facility, not memorized phrases |
| Data interpretation | PBL students work with real data regularly; exam data problems are straightforward |

### The Transfer Advantage

Traditional students learn content in isolation and must transfer it to exam contexts. PBL students learn content through application and must transfer it to exam formats. The second transfer is smaller and easier.

## The 高考 Readiness Timeline

### Grades 1-9 (Ages 6-15): Foundation Building

Knowledge is acquired through PBL projects. Coverage is tracked against curriculum standards (see mapping documents). Monthly diagnostics identify and fill gaps.

**By end of Grade 9** (中考): Students should meet or exceed curriculum benchmarks across all subjects.

### Grade 10 (Age 16): Capstone + Subject Deepening

Major capstone project. Subjects deepen through advanced projects. Knowledge coverage continues through PBL.

### Grade 11, Semester 1 (Age 17 fall): Advanced Capstone + Exam Awareness

Complete capstone project. Begin explicit 高考 format familiarization:
- Students study past 高考 papers not as tests but as "problems to analyze"
- "What is this question actually testing? What knowledge and skills does it require?"
- Students map their project experience to exam topics: "I already know this from when we..."

### Grade 11, Semester 2 (Age 17 spring): The Gear Shift

**This is the critical transition.** Projects become more exam-aligned:
- Math projects include formal problem sets in exam format
- 语文 projects include timed essay practice
- Science projects include standardized problem-solving components
- The GAP AUDIT happens: systematic identification of any knowledge points not fully covered through PBL

**Gap Sprint** (4-6 weeks): Targeted, intensive work on any remaining gaps. For well-designed PBL curricula, this should be a short list.

### Grade 12, Semester 1 (Age 18 fall): Strategic Exam Preparation

Parallel tracks:
1. **Content mastery verification**: Subject-by-subject review using PBL understanding as the foundation
2. **Format training**: Practice exam-specific formats, timing strategies, question-type approaches
3. **Diagnostic testing**: Monthly full-length or section-length practice exams
4. **Weakness targeting**: AI-assisted identification of weak areas → focused practice

### Grade 12, Semester 2 (Age 18 spring): Intensive Exam Preparation

Full exam preparation mode:
- Weekly practice exams (timed, full format)
- Review sessions focused on common error patterns
- Strategy optimization: which questions to attempt first, time allocation, etc.
- Stress management and performance optimization

**But with a difference**: PBL students enter this period with:
- Deep understanding (not shallow memorization that needs refreshing)
- Confidence from real accomplishments ("I've solved harder problems")
- Self-knowledge from years of journaling ("I know how I learn best")
- Agency ("I'm choosing this strategy" vs. "I'm doing what teacher says")
- Resilience from years of productive failure handling

## Subject-Specific Readiness Strategies

### Math

**PBL Advantage**: Students understand what math IS FOR. They've used every concept.

**Exam-Specific Training Needed**:
- Speed and accuracy on routine calculations (AI-assisted timed practice)
- Formal proof writing conventions
- Exam-specific problem types (选择题 strategies, 解答题 format)
- Common traps and edge cases

**Training Method**: "Exam Kata" — daily 20-minute practice sessions on specific problem types. Not boring drill — positioned as "strategy game against the test designers."

### 语文

**PBL Advantage**: Students write well, read deeply, and have genuine analytical capacity.

**Exam-Specific Training Needed**:
- Timed essay writing (800+ words in ~45 minutes)
- Multiple-choice format for reading comprehension
- Specific 文言文 vocabulary and grammar patterns tested frequently
- Poetry appreciation in exam format (specific question types)

**Training Method**: Weekly timed essays + review. 文言文 vocabulary cards (spaced repetition). Past paper analysis as a research project: "What do the test designers value?"

### English

**PBL Advantage**: Students have used English for real research and communication.

**Exam-Specific Training Needed**:
- Cloze test format
- Grammar point drills for tested patterns
- Listening test format familiarization
- Writing template awareness (not dependence)

### Sciences (Physics, Chemistry, Biology)

**PBL Advantage**: Students understand concepts through application. Lab experience is genuine.

**Exam-Specific Training Needed**:
- Formula recall and rapid application
- Standardized calculation methods
- Multiple-choice elimination strategies
- Exam-specific experimental analysis formats

**Training Method**: Subject-specific problem sets organized by topic and difficulty. Start from PBL context → abstract to exam format → practice in exam conditions.

### Readiness Milestones

| Milestone | When | Standard |

| Exam Confidence | 2 weeks before 高考 | Student reports feeling prepared; stress manageable |

## The Honest Risks

See `risk/risk-analysis.md` for detailed analysis. Key exam-related risks:

1. **Coverage gaps**: Some knowledge points may be insufficiently covered through PBL → mitigation: rigorous tracking + gap sprints
2. **Format unfamiliarity**: PBL doesn't naturally produce exam format skills → mitigation: dedicated format training in Grade 11-12
3. **Speed deficits**: PBL values depth over speed; exams reward speed → mitigation: timed practice regimen
4. **Parent anxiety**: Parents may not trust PBL until exam results prove it → mitigation: transparent tracking + early diagnostic evidence
