# Math: Knowledge Point → Problem Mapping

## Mapping Approach

Every math concept in the 高考 syllabus is mapped to one or more real-world problems. Math is perhaps the easiest subject to transform — every concept was invented to solve a real problem.

---

## Elementary (Grades 1-6, Explorer + Builder Stages)

### 1. Addition/Subtraction (Grades 1-2)

**Knowledge Point**: Operations within 100, then 10,000; word problems

**PBL Problem**: "You have ¥50 to plan a class snack party. Here's the price list. Can everyone get something? How much is left over?"

**Deeper Version**: "Compare prices at three stores. Which store gives the best deal for our party?"

**主体性 Marker**: Student makes purchasing decisions, calculates independently, presents plan to class.

---

### 2. Multiplication Tables 九九乘法表 (Grade 2-3)

**Knowledge Point**: Memorize multiplication facts, apply to word problems

**PBL Problem**: "Design a seating arrangement for the school assembly. There are 6 rows of 8 chairs. How many students can sit? What if we need 72 seats?"

**Supplementary**: Multiplication fluency practice via games (not worksheets). AI-generated adaptive practice for speed.

**主体性 Marker**: "I can calculate how many we need without counting one by one."

---

### 3. Fractions and Decimals (Grades 3-5)

**Knowledge Point**: Fraction operations, fraction-decimal conversion, comparison

**PBL Problem**: "You're making 饺子 for 20 people. The recipe serves 4. Scale it up. How much of each ingredient? The store sells flour in 500g bags — how many bags?"

**Deeper Version**: "Compare nutritional information on food labels. Which snack has the highest proportion of protein? Express as fractions and percentages."

**主体性 Marker**: Student modifies recipes independently, explains scaling logic.

---

### 4. Geometry — Area, Perimeter, Volume (Grades 4-6)

**Knowledge Point**: Calculate area/perimeter of rectangles, triangles, circles; volume of prisms

**PBL Problem**: "Design a garden for the school courtyard. You have 30m² of space. Include at least 3 different shaped beds. Calculate the planting area and the path area. How much soil do you need (volume)?"

**Deeper Version**: "Which design maximizes planting area? Is a circle or square bed more efficient?"

**主体性 Marker**: Student presents garden design with all calculations justified.

---

### 5. Ratio and Proportion (Grade 6)

**Knowledge Point**: Ratio, proportion, scale drawing, unit rate

**PBL Problem**: "Create a scale model of your classroom (or bedroom). Choose a scale. Calculate all dimensions. Build it from cardboard."

**Deeper Version**: "The school wants to enlarge a photo for the hallway. The original is 10cm × 15cm. The wall space is 1.2m wide. What's the largest size that maintains proportion?"

---

## Middle School (Grades 7-9, Investigator Stage)

### 6. Algebraic Expressions and Equations (Grade 7)

**Knowledge Point**: Variables, expressions, solving linear equations

**PBL Problem**: "A phone plan costs ¥39/month plus ¥0.1/MB for data. Another costs ¥69/month with 5GB included, then ¥0.05/MB. At what usage level does Plan B become cheaper? Express as an equation and solve."

**Deeper Version**: "Survey classmates on data usage. Recommend the best plan for different user types. Present with mathematical justification."

**主体性 Marker**: "I can figure out which deal is actually better — the ads are misleading."

---

### 7. Functions — Linear (Grade 8)

**Knowledge Point**: Linear functions, slope, intercept, graphing, applications

**PBL Problem**: "Track the temperature in our classroom every hour for a week. Is there a pattern? Can you model it? Predict tomorrow's temperature at 2pm."

**Deeper Version**: "A local business's revenue grows by ¥2,000/month. They started at ¥10,000. When will they break even if costs are ¥18,000/month? What if growth isn't linear?"

---

### 8. Geometry Proofs — Triangles, Congruence, Similarity (Grades 8-9)

**Knowledge Point**: Congruent/similar triangles, proofs, Pythagorean theorem

**PBL Problem**: "Without crossing the river, determine its width. You only have a measuring tape and stakes. Design and execute the measurement."

**How it works**: Students discover similar triangles as the tool needed to solve the problem. The proof emerges from the need to justify their method.

**主体性 Marker**: "I can measure something I can't reach — using math."

---

### 9. Quadratic Functions (Grade 9)

**Knowledge Point**: Parabolas, vertex, axis of symmetry, optimization

**PBL Problem**: "Design a water fountain. The water follows a parabolic path. Given the pump pressure (initial velocity), what angle maximizes the distance? What angle creates the tallest arc?"

**Alternative**: "A farmer has 40m of fencing to build a rectangular pen against a wall. What dimensions maximize the area?"

**Deeper Version**: "A ticket seller finds that for every ¥10 price increase, 20 fewer people attend. Current price: ¥80, attendance: 500. What price maximizes revenue?"

---

### 10. Probability and Statistics (Grades 7-9)

**Knowledge Point**: Data collection, mean/median/mode, probability experiments

**PBL Problem**: "Is the school vending machine fair? Track outcomes of 200 purchases. Does every slot have equal probability? Design and conduct the investigation."

**Alternative**: "Conduct a survey on student sleep habits. Analyze the data. What recommendations would you make to the school? Support with statistics."

---

## High School (Grades 10-12, Architect Stage)

### 11. Functions — Exponential and Logarithmic (Grade 10)

**Knowledge Point**: Exponential/log functions, properties, equations

**PBL Problem**: "COVID-19 spread followed exponential growth initially. Given data from the first 30 days of an outbreak, model the growth. When would the hospital system be overwhelmed? What does 'flattening the curve' mean mathematically?"

**Alternative**: "How long does it take for an investment to double at different interest rates? Derive the Rule of 72."

**主体性 Marker**: "I can model real phenomena and make predictions — not just plug numbers into formulas."

---

### 12. Trigonometry (Grade 10)

**Knowledge Point**: Trig functions, identities, graphs, applications

**PBL Problem**: "Design a solar panel installation. At our latitude, what angle maximizes energy capture across seasons? How does this change throughout the year? Create an optimization model."

**Alternative**: "Measure the height of the school building using only a protractor, measuring tape, and trigonometry. Then verify with actual blueprints."

---

### 13. Sequences and Series (Grade 11)

**Knowledge Point**: Arithmetic/geometric sequences, sum formulas

**PBL Problem**: "Design a savings plan. If you save ¥100/month with 3% annual interest compounded monthly, how much do you have after 10 years? Compare: what if you increase savings by ¥10/month each year?"

**Alternative**: "The legend of the chessboard: 1 grain of rice on the first square, 2 on the second, 4 on the third... Is there enough rice in the world?"

---

### 14. Analytic Geometry — Conic Sections (Grade 11-12)

**Knowledge Point**: Circles, ellipses, parabolas, hyperbolas in coordinate system

**PBL Problem**: "Satellite orbits are elliptical. Given Earth's radius and the satellite's closest/farthest distances, write the equation of the orbit. Calculate the satellite's speed at different points."

**Alternative**: "Design a parabolic solar cooker. What focal length maximizes heat concentration for a given diameter?"

---

### 15. Calculus Basics — Derivatives (Grade 12)

**Knowledge Point**: Limits intuition, derivative concept, rules, applications to optimization

**PBL Problem**: "A factory produces cans. Material cost depends on surface area; storage cost depends on volume. For a fixed volume of 330mL, what dimensions minimize total cost? Use calculus to prove your answer is optimal."

**Deeper Version**: "The factory changes its can shape. Compare cylindrical, rectangular, and other cross-sections. Which is most material-efficient? Why do most cans look the way they do?"

**主体性 Marker**: "I can optimize real designs — I understand why things are shaped the way they are."
