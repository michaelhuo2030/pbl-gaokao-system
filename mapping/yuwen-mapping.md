# 语文: Knowledge Point → Problem Mapping

## The Challenge

语文 (Chinese Language and Literature) is uniquely challenging to transform because it combines:
- Mechanical skills (character recognition, writing, grammar)
- Literary appreciation (poetry, classical Chinese, modern literature)
- Production skills (essay writing in specific formats)
- Cultural knowledge (literary history, classical allusions)

The traditional approach treats these as separate drills. PBL integrates them through **authentic language use**.

## The Principle

> People learn language by using language for purposes they care about.

Every 语文 skill is acquired through projects where students READ because they want to understand, WRITE because they have something to say, and encounter 文言文 because they need to access historical sources.

---

## Elementary (Grades 1-6)

### 1. Character Recognition and Writing (Grades 1-3)

**Knowledge Point**: ~1600 recognition / ~800 writing characters by Grade 3

**PBL Problem**: "Publish a class newspaper every two weeks."

**How it works**:
- Students write articles about topics they choose (school events, nature observations, family stories)
- Characters are learned because students NEED them for their articles
- New characters are introduced in context: "You want to write about 蝴蝶 (butterfly)? Here's how."
- Peer editing requires reading each other's work — character recognition through real reading
- Word walls grow organically from project vocabulary, not from textbook lists

**Supplementary**: Daily 10-minute character practice focused on project-relevant characters. AI-assisted spaced repetition.

**主体性 Marker**: "I wrote this article. These are my words."

---

### 2. Reading Comprehension (Grades 2-6)

**Knowledge Point**: Understand main ideas, identify details, make inferences, analyze author's purpose

**PBL Problem**: "Book Club Detective — Read a book, then investigate: Why did the author write this? What did they want us to feel? Prepare a case file."

**How it works**:
- Students choose from curated book lists (aligned with curriculum reading requirements)
- Instead of answering comprehension questions, they build "investigation files"
- They present their findings to the class and defend their interpretations
- Peer questions drive deeper analysis than any worksheet

**Alternative**: "Fact-Check Challenge — Here's a news article. What claims does it make? Can you verify them? Write a fact-check report."

---

### 3. Writing — 记叙文 (Narrative) (Grades 3-6)

**Knowledge Point**: Write narratives with clear structure, vivid details, personal voice

**PBL Problem**: "Personal Story Collection — Interview a family member about a memorable experience. Write their story. Compile class stories into a book."

**How it works**:
- Students practice interviewing (listening + speaking skills)
- They write narratives about real people and real events
- Peer editing workshops focus on "Does this feel real? Can I see it?"
- The published collection creates real audience and purpose

**主体性 Marker**: "I preserved my grandmother's story. She cried when she read it."

---

### 4. Ancient Poetry 古诗 (Grades 1-6)

**Knowledge Point**: Memorize ~75 poems, understand meaning, appreciate imagery

**PBL Problem**: "Poetry Expedition — Find a poem that matches [this photo / this season / this feeling]. Present it, explain why it fits, and write your own poem inspired by it."

**How it works**:
- Instead of "memorize this poem," students search through collections to find matches
- They encounter many more poems than required while searching
- The poem they choose is memorable because THEY chose it
- Writing their own poem deepens understanding of form and imagery

**Alternative**: "Poetry Across Time — Pair an ancient poem with a modern song that expresses the same feeling. Present both and analyze the connection."

## Middle School (Grades 7-9, Investigator Stage)

### 5. 文言文 (Classical Chinese) (Grades 7-9)

**Knowledge Point**: Read and translate classical texts, understand grammar patterns, key vocabulary

**PBL Problem**: "Historical Mystery — A historical event is debated. Read the primary sources (文言文) and secondary accounts. Which version is most credible? Write your analysis."

**How it works**:
- Students encounter 文言文 because they NEED to read original documents
- Translation is motivated: "What does this official report actually say about the event?"
- Grammar patterns (之、者、也、焉、乎) are learned through repeated exposure in context
- Students build their own 文言文 vocabulary lists from what they encounter

**Alternative**: "法律考古 — Ancient Chinese legal cases. Read the original case record (文言文). What was the dispute? How was it decided? Would we decide it differently today?"

**主体性 Marker**: "I can read a 1000-year-old document and understand what it says."

---

### 6. 说明文 (Expository Writing) (Grades 7-8)

**Knowledge Point**: Write clear explanatory texts with logical structure, use data and examples

**PBL Problem**: "Science Communication Challenge — Take a complex topic from your science class and write an explanation that a 5th grader can understand. Test it on actual 5th graders."

**How it works**:
- Students must truly understand the topic to explain it simply
- The real audience (younger students) provides genuine feedback
- Structure, clarity, and use of examples are tested in practice, not by rubric alone

---

### 7. 议论文 (Argumentative Writing) (Grades 8-9)

**Knowledge Point**: Write arguments with clear thesis, supporting evidence, counterargument consideration

**PBL Problem**: "School Policy Debate — The school is considering [real policy change]. Write a position paper arguing for or against it. Present to the student council."

**How it works**:
- The topic is real and affects students directly
- They must research, gather evidence, and anticipate counterarguments
- The audience (student council, administration) is real
- Best papers may actually influence policy

**Alternative**: "Editorial Board — Write opinion pieces for the school newspaper on topics students choose. Editorial board (peers) decides what gets published based on quality of argument."

---

### 8. 名著导读 (Required Literature) (Grades 7-9)

**Knowledge Point**: Read and analyze designated novels (e.g., 《西游记》《水浒传》《朝花夕拾》《骆驼祥子》)

**PBL Problem**: "Book on Trial — Put a character from [required novel] on trial. Was their key decision justified? Prosecution and defense must cite the text."

**How it works**:
- Deep reading is required to build a legal case
- Students engage with character motivation, theme, and context
- The adversarial format ensures multiple perspectives
- Text citation becomes natural, not forced

**Alternative**: "Adaptation Project — Adapt one chapter of a 名著 into a different medium (radio play, graphic novel, short film script). Justify your creative choices based on the original text."

---

## High School (Grades 10-12, Architect Stage)

### 9. 高考 Essay (800+ words) (Grades 10-12)

**Knowledge Point**: Write a well-structured essay on a given prompt within time constraints, demonstrating depth of thought and cultural literacy

**PBL Problem**: "Weekly Think Piece — Choose a topic from current events, philosophy, or personal observation. Write 800 words. Peer review circle provides feedback. Best pieces published in school journal."

**How it works**:
- Students write frequently on topics they care about → builds writing fluency
- Peer review develops critical reading of arguments
- Published work creates stakes and audience
- By Grade 12, students have written 100+ essays — the exam essay is routine

**Exam Synthesis**: In Grade 12, practice shifts to timed responses to 高考-style prompts. But the writing muscle is already built.

**主体性 Marker**: "I have things to say and I know how to say them. The exam essay is just another one."

---

### 10. Advanced 文言文 and Literary Analysis (Grades 10-12)

**Knowledge Point**: Translate unseen 文言文 passages, analyze literary techniques in modern and classical works

**PBL Problem**: "Intellectual History Project — Trace one idea (e.g., 仁, 自由, 天命) through Chinese intellectual history. Read original texts from different periods. How did the concept evolve?"

**How it works**:
- Students read 文言文 across periods and styles — far more exposure than textbook excerpts
- They develop genuine facility through volume and purpose
- The analytical skill transfers directly to exam questions
