# Science: Knowledge Point → Problem Mapping

## Subjects Covered

- **Physics** (Grades 8-12)
- **Chemistry** (Grades 9-12)
- **Biology** (Grades 7-12)

## The Natural Advantage

Science is the subject most naturally suited to PBL. Every scientific concept was discovered by someone trying to understand or solve a real problem. The transformation is often just: restore the original context.

## Physics

### Grade 8-9 (Investigator Stage)

#### 1. Mechanics — Force and Motion

**Knowledge Point**: Newton's laws, friction, gravity, free-body diagrams

**PBL Problem**: "Egg Drop Challenge 2.0 — Design a container that protects an egg dropped from the 3rd floor. But also: predict EXACTLY when it will hit the ground. Use physics, not just padding."

**How it works**:
- Students must understand gravity, air resistance, and force to predict landing time
- Free-body diagrams emerge from the need to analyze forces on their design
- Newton's laws aren't memorized — they're discovered through testing predictions
- Failed designs teach more than successful ones

**Alternative**: "Why does the bus make you fall forward when it brakes? Explain with physics, then design a safer bus handrail system."

---

#### 2. Optics

**Knowledge Point**: Reflection, refraction, lenses, image formation

**PBL Problem**: "Camera Obscura Lab — Build a working camera from a box. Explain why the image is inverted. Then design improvements: how would you make the image brighter? Sharper?"

**主体性 Marker**: "I built a camera from a box and I can explain why it works."

---

#### 3. Electricity

**Knowledge Point**: Circuits, Ohm's law, series/parallel, power, safety

**PBL Problem**: "Wire a Model House — Design and build the electrical system for a model house. Include lighting for 3 rooms, a switch for each, and calculate the total power consumption. Is your wiring safe?"

**Deeper Version**: "The school's electricity bill is ¥8000/month. Audit the school's electrical usage. Where is energy wasted? Propose savings and calculate the impact."

---

### Grade 10-12 (Architect Stage)

#### 4. Kinematics and Dynamics (Advanced)

**Knowledge Point**: Projectile motion, circular motion, gravitational field, energy conservation

**PBL Problem**: "Sports Physics Lab — Analyze the physics of a basketball free throw (or soccer kick, or badminton smash). Film it, measure trajectories, calculate forces. What makes a perfect shot? Build a model."

**Alternative**: "Roller Coaster Design — Design a roller coaster track. Calculate speeds at every point using energy conservation. Where are the maximum G-forces? Is your design safe for riders?"

---

#### 5. Electromagnetism

**Knowledge Point**: Electric fields, magnetic fields, electromagnetic induction, AC/DC

**PBL Problem**: "Build a Generator — Construct a simple electric generator. Measure its output. How does rotation speed affect voltage? How does coil count affect it? Derive the relationship mathematically."

**Deeper Version**: "Compare energy generation methods (solar, wind, hydro, nuclear) for our region. Which is most feasible? Calculate energy output using physics principles."

---

#### 6. Waves and Modern Physics

**Knowledge Point**: Wave properties, sound, light spectrum, photoelectric effect, nuclear physics

**PBL Problem**: "Sound Engineering Project — Design the acoustics for the school auditorium. Why do some seats hear well and others don't? Use wave physics to propose improvements."

**Alternative**: "The Photoelectric Effect Mystery — Recreate Hertz's observation. Why does light eject electrons but only above a certain frequency? Design experiments, then research how Einstein explained it."

## Chemistry

### Grade 9 (Investigator Stage)

#### 7. Basic Chemistry — Elements, Compounds, Reactions

**Knowledge Point**: Periodic table, chemical formulas, reaction types, balancing equations

**PBL Problem**: "Kitchen Chemistry Investigation — Why does baking soda + vinegar bubble? Why does iron rust? Why does cut apple turn brown? Investigate 5 kitchen reactions. Identify reaction types and write balanced equations."

**主体性 Marker**: "I can explain what's actually happening when I cook — it's chemistry."

---

#### 8. Acids, Bases, and Solutions

**Knowledge Point**: pH, neutralization, indicators, solution concentration

**PBL Problem**: "Water Quality Inspector — Test water samples from 5 sources (tap, bottled, rain, river, pool). Measure pH, dissolved solids, and other properties. Which is safest to drink? Write a public health report."

---

### Grade 10-12 (Architect Stage)

#### 9. Organic Chemistry

**Knowledge Point**: Hydrocarbons, functional groups, polymers, biochemistry basics

**PBL Problem**: "Plastic Audit — What are the plastics in our daily life made of? Classify them by polymer type. Which are recyclable? Design a school plastic reduction strategy based on chemistry."

**Alternative**: "Drug Design Basics — How does aspirin work? Start from willow bark (historical context), trace the chemistry of synthesis and mechanism. Why do some molecules work as medicine and others don't?"

---

#### 10. Chemical Equilibrium and Reaction Rates

**Knowledge Point**: Le Chatelier's principle, catalysts, equilibrium constants

**PBL Problem**: "Industrial Optimization — The Haber process makes ammonia for fertilizer. How do temperature, pressure, and catalysts affect yield? If you ran the factory, what conditions would you choose? The economics matter too."

**Deeper Version**: "Why do we need fertilizer at all? Trace the nitrogen cycle, the chemistry of soil, and the Haber process. What are the environmental costs?"

---

## Biology

### Grade 7-9 (Investigator Stage)

#### 11. Cell Biology

**Knowledge Point**: Cell structure, organelles, cell division, microscopy

**PBL Problem**: "Microscope Investigation — Prepare and examine at least 5 different cell types. Document differences. Why do cells look different? What does form tell us about function? Create an illustrated field guide."

---

#### 12. Human Body Systems

**Knowledge Point**: Digestive, circulatory, respiratory, nervous systems

**PBL Problem**: "Health Advisor — A classmate wants to improve their athletic performance. Analyze what happens in the body during exercise (all systems). Design a training and nutrition plan based on biology."

---

#### 13. Ecology

**Knowledge Point**: Ecosystems, food webs, energy flow, biodiversity

**PBL Problem**: "Ecosystem Audit — Survey the biodiversity of the school grounds or a nearby park. Map the food web. What would happen if one species disappeared? Present findings as a conservation report."

---

### Grade 10-12 (Architect Stage)

#### 14. Genetics and Evolution

**Knowledge Point**: DNA, genes, Mendelian genetics, evolution, natural selection

**PBL Problem**: "Genetic Counselor Simulation — A family has a hereditary condition (case study). Trace the inheritance pattern. Calculate probabilities for future children. What genetic testing options exist? Write a counseling brief."

**Deeper Version**: "CRISPR Ethics — You can now edit genes. Should you? Research the technology, examine case studies, write a position paper with biological AND ethical arguments."

---

#### 15. Molecular Biology

**Knowledge Point**: DNA replication, transcription, translation, gene expression

**PBL Problem**: "Protein Factory — Model the journey from DNA to protein. Build a physical or digital model. Then: what happens when there's a mutation? Model 3 types of mutations and predict their effects."
