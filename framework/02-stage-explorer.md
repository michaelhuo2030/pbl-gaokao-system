# Stage 2: Explorer (Ages 6-9, Grades 1-3)

## Developmental Reality

Children are now capable of structured thinking, reading, writing, and sustained projects. They still learn best through concrete, hands-on experience. Abstract reasoning is emerging but not dominant. Social comparison begins — motivation architecture matters.

## Core Mode: Structured Curiosity Through First Projects

Short projects (1-2 weeks) centered on questions children naturally ask. Formal skills (reading, writing, arithmetic) are acquired as tools needed for projects.

## What 体制内 Requires (Grades 1-3)

### 语文
- Pinyin mastery, ~1600 characters recognition, ~800 characters writing
- Reading comprehension (short passages)
- Writing: sentences → short paragraphs
- Classical poetry memorization (古诗)

### Math
- Addition/subtraction within 10,000
- Multiplication tables (九九乘法表)
- Basic geometry (shapes, perimeter)
- Time, measurement, money
- Word problems

### English
- Basic vocabulary, phonics
- Simple sentences, greetings

### Science (道德与法治 / 科学)
- Basic natural phenomena
- Simple experiments
- Social rules, community awareness

## The Transformation: Curriculum → Problems

### 语文 Example

**Traditional**: Read text, answer comprehension questions, write characters 10 times each.

**PBL Explorer**:
- **Project**: "We're writing a class newspaper about our neighborhood."
- Students interview neighbors, write short articles, edit each other's work
- Character learning happens through words they NEED for their articles
- 古诗 is encountered through a "Poetry Detective" challenge: "Find a poem that matches this photo you took outside"
- Reading comprehension: they read each other's articles and ask questions

The character count and reading level requirements are met. The WAY they're met builds agency.

### Math Example

**Traditional**: Drill multiplication tables, do 50 practice problems.

**PBL Explorer**:
- **Project**: "Design a snack shop for the school fair."
- Students calculate prices, make change, figure out how many items they need
- Multiplication emerges from "We need 6 bags of 8 cookies each — how many cookies total?"
- Geometry: "Design the layout of your shop table. How much space do you have?"
- Data: "Track what sold. What was most popular? Make a chart."

九九乘法表 is still memorized — but now students know why multiplication exists.

### Science Example

**Traditional**: "Water has three states: solid, liquid, gas."

**PBL Explorer**:
- **Project**: "Investigation: Where does the puddle go?"
- Students observe a puddle over days, measure it, record changes
- They discover evaporation through observation before being given the word
- Extension: "Can you make water disappear faster? Slower? Design an experiment."
- They encounter states of matter because they need the concepts to explain what they saw

## Weekly Structure (Sample)

| Day | Morning (语文 + Math block) | Afternoon (Project + Specials) |
|---|---|---|
| Mon | Project launch: framing the question + skills needed | Research / exploration / field work |
| Tue | Guided skill work (characters, calculations needed for project) | Building / creating / experimenting |
| Wed | Reading + writing workshop (project-connected texts) | Peer review + revision |
| Thu | Math problem-solving (project-connected challenges) | Documentation + presentation prep |
| Fri | Skills practice + reflection journal | Presentations + "What did we figure out?" |

## 主体性 at Ages 6-9

Agency shifts from physical ("I can do it myself") to cognitive ("I can figure it out"):

- **"我可以调查..."** (I can investigate...) — conducting simple research
- **"我的想法是..."** (My idea is...) — proposing hypotheses
- **"我做到了因为..."** (I did it because...) — articulating process, not just result

### Concrete Practices

1. **Investigation journals**: Each student keeps a notebook of questions and findings. Not graded — celebrated.
2. **Choice within structure**: "This week's math challenge has three versions. Pick the one that interests you."
3. **Teach-backs**: Students teach one concept to a peer each week. You don't understand it until you can teach it.
4. **Mistake celebrations**: "Most interesting mistake of the week" — normalize productive failure.
5. **Portfolio, not just tests**: Students collect their best work and reflect: "This shows I can..."

## AI Integration

**AI is still mostly invisible**, but beginning to appear as a tool:

- Students can ask AI questions during research ("What kind of animal is this?") with adult guidance
- AI is positioned as "one source" — students also use books, observation, people
- **Critical rule**: AI never does the work. It answers questions the student formulates.
- Teachers/parents use AI to generate differentiated problem sets based on where each child is

## Assessment

### What Replaces Traditional Tests

| Traditional | PBL Explorer |
|---|---|
| Character dictation test | Characters correctly used in published article |
| Math speed drill | Math correctly applied in project calculations |
| Reading comprehension quiz | Comprehension demonstrated in discussion and project work |

### 高考 Alignment Check

Every 6 weeks, a brief diagnostic covers curriculum knowledge points. Not high-stakes — diagnostic only. Gaps feed into the next project cycle's skill targets.

## What Success Looks Like at Age 9

- [ ] Reads age-appropriate texts fluently and discusses content with opinions
- [ ] Writes coherent paragraphs with purpose (not just "because teacher said")
- [ ] Has automated arithmetic facts AND understands when to use them
- [ ] Has conducted at least 10 mini-investigations and can describe the process
- [ ] Can collaborate in a small group toward a shared goal
- [ ] Says "让我想想" (Let me think about it) before "老师怎么说的?" (What did the teacher say?)
- [ ] Has a portfolio of work they're proud of
- [ ] Views learning as exploration, not compliance
