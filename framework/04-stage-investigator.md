# Stage 4: Investigator (Ages 13-15, Grades 7-9 / 初中)

## Developmental Reality

Adolescence. Identity formation is the dominant force. Students ask "Who am I?" and "Why does any of this matter?" Abstract reasoning is fully available. Social dynamics intensify. This is where traditional education most often kills motivation — and where PBL has the highest potential impact.

This stage ends with 中考 (zhongkao) — a real, high-stakes exam that determines high school placement. The system must prepare students for it.

## Core Mode: Research-Driven Projects With Real Stakes

Projects span 4-8 weeks. Students tackle problems with genuine complexity and ambiguity. Work products increasingly resemble what adults actually produce: reports, prototypes, presentations with evidence, data analysis.

## What 体制内 Requires (Grades 7-9)

### 语文
- 3500+ characters, complex text analysis
- Essay writing: 议论文 (argumentative), 记叙文, 说明文
- 文言文 reading and translation
- Extensive literature (名著导读)
- Advanced poetry analysis

### Math
- Algebra (equations, inequalities, functions)
- Geometry (proofs, congruence, similarity, circles)
- Statistics and probability
- Coordinate geometry
- Quadratic functions

### English
- Reading comprehension, grammar, writing
- Listening, speaking assessments

### Physics (from Grade 8)
- Mechanics, optics, heat, electricity, magnetism

### Chemistry (from Grade 9)
- Elements, compounds, reactions, acids/bases

### Biology
- Cells, organisms, ecology, human body systems

### History & Geography
- Chinese history, world history
- Physical and human geography

### 道德与法治 (Politics)
- Law, governance, social ethics

## The Transformation: Frontier Problems

At this age, problems should feel **real and consequential**. Not toy problems — frontier problems.

### "Energy Transition" Project (8 weeks)

**Mission**: "China plans to reach carbon neutrality by 2060. Your district has been asked to propose an energy transition plan. Design it."

**Subjects covered**:
- **Physics**: Energy conversion, electricity, thermal efficiency
- **Chemistry**: Combustion, renewable energy chemistry, battery chemistry
- **Math**: Functions (modeling energy demand over time), statistics (analyzing consumption data), linear programming
- **Geography**: Resource distribution, climate zones, urban/rural differences
- **语文**: Write policy proposal (议论文), present to simulated review panel
- **English**: Read international energy reports
- **Politics**: Environmental law, governance structures

**高考-relevant knowledge**: Function modeling, chemical equations, energy physics, argumentative writing — all in one project.

### "Genetic Detective" Project (6 weeks)

**Mission**: "A family has a hereditary condition. Trace the genetics, explain the mechanism, and research current treatment approaches."

**Subjects covered**:
- **Biology**: Genetics, heredity, DNA, cell division
- **Math**: Probability (Mendelian genetics ratios)
- **Chemistry**: Molecular structure basics
- **语文**: Write explanatory report, read scientific texts
- **Ethics (道德与法治)**: Genetic privacy, healthcare access

### "History on Trial" Project (5 weeks)

**Mission**: "A historical decision is controversial. Stage a trial. Prosecution and defense must use primary sources."

- **History**: Deep understanding of a period through adversarial analysis
- **语文**: 文言文 (reading primary sources!), argumentative writing and speaking
- **Politics**: Legal reasoning, evidence standards
- **English**: Compare international historians' perspectives

## Journaling: The Investigator's Log

Journaling evolves into a research and reflection practice:

### Structure

1. **Sensing**: "What am I noticing about this problem / about myself this week?" (Presencing practice)
2. **Hypothesis**: "What I think is going on and why"
3. **Evidence**: "What I found that supports or contradicts my thinking"
4. **Revision**: "How my understanding changed"
5. **Agency**: "What I now know I can do that I couldn't before"
6. **Open loops**: "Questions I still have"

The journal becomes a **thinking tool**, not a diary. Students review their journals to see their own intellectual growth — concrete evidence that "我可以" is real.

### 80/20 in Journal Review

When mentors review journals, 80% of attention goes to motivation signals:
- What lights this student up?
- Where is energy draining?
- What problems do they naturally pursue beyond requirements?

20% goes to knowledge gaps and skill needs.

## 中考 Integration

### The Honest Conversation

At age 13, students and families get a transparent framework:

> "中考 is real and important. Here's how our approach prepares you. Here's what we add in the final semester to ensure you're exam-ready."

### Strategy

1. **Years 7-8**: PBL-driven learning covers 85%+ of knowledge points naturally through projects
2. **Year 9, Semester 1**: Projects continue but become more exam-aligned (problem formats mirror exam questions)
3. **Year 9, Semester 2**: Focused exam preparation — but from a position of deep understanding
   - Students who understand physics through real problems learn exam tricks faster
   - 文言文 encountered through primary-source research is retained better than through rote

### Gap Analysis Protocol

Every semester, diagnostic assessments map each student against 中考 standards. Gaps feed directly into project design:
- If 30% of students are weak on circle geometry → next project includes a design challenge requiring circles
- If 文言文 comprehension is lagging → next history project requires more primary source work

## 主体性 at Ages 13-15

Agency becomes identity-level:

- **"我是那种会..."** (I'm the kind of person who...) — self-concept as investigator/builder
- **"我不同意，因为..."** (I disagree because...) — intellectual independence
- **"这个问题重要因为..."** (This problem matters because...) — connecting learning to values

### Concrete Practices

1. **Personal inquiry projects**: Each student runs one self-chosen investigation per semester alongside group projects
2. **Mentor meetings**: Bi-weekly 1:1 with a mentor to discuss journal, progress, and direction
3. **Public defense**: Major projects end with a public presentation where students field hard questions
4. **Cross-age mentoring**: Investigators mentor Builders (ages 10-12) — teaching deepens understanding
5. **Intellectual autobiography**: End-of-year essay: "What I learned about how I learn" — metacognitive capstone

## AI Integration

**AI as thinking partner and tool for augmentation:**

- Students use AI for research synthesis, brainstorming, and draft feedback
- **AI critique sessions**: Students evaluate AI-generated essays/solutions against their own work
- Students learn to prompt effectively: "How do I get AI to help me think, not think for me?"
- **Transparency rule**: All AI use is documented in project reports ("AI assisted with X; I verified by Y")
- Introduction of AI-augmented data analysis for project work

## Assessment

### Project Portfolio (70% of evaluation)
- Quality of research, analysis, and artifact
- Process documentation (journal evidence)
- Public defense performance

### Diagnostic Checks (20%)
- Monthly curriculum-aligned diagnostics
- Student-led gap analysis: "Here's where I'm strong; here's what I need to work on"

### Self-Assessment (10%)
- Journal-based reflection
- 主体性 self-evaluation: "What can I do now?"

### 中考 Readiness (Year 9)
- Twice-monthly practice exams (timed, full format)
- Analysis sessions: not just "what did you get wrong" but "why does this question work this way?"

## What Success Looks Like at Age 15

- [ ] Can conduct independent research on a complex topic over multiple weeks
- [ ] Writes argumentative, expository, and narrative essays with genuine voice
- [ ] Applies mathematical concepts as analytical tools, not just exercise answers
- [ ] Understands science through investigation, not just memorization
- [ ] Uses AI productively and critically
- [ ] Maintains a substantive journal showing intellectual growth
- [ ] Has an identity as a thinker/investigator, not just a student
- [ ] Performs at or above grade level on 中考 diagnostics
- [ ] Can articulate why what they're learning matters — to them personally
