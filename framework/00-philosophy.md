# Design Philosophy

## The Synthesis, Not the Compromise

This system does not compromise between PBL and 高考. It synthesizes them.

The insight: the knowledge demanded by China's national curriculum is not arbitrary. It represents genuine human understanding — mathematics, language, science, history, geography. The problem was never *what* students learn. The problem is *how* they encounter it.

When a student encounters quadratic equations as "page 47, exercise 3-15," they learn to solve quadratic equations. When they encounter them as "how do I maximize the yield of my garden plot," they learn to solve quadratic equations AND they learn what mathematics is *for*. The exam result is the same. The student is different.

## Six Pillars

### 1. Problem-First, Always

No topic is introduced in the abstract. Every concept arrives because a problem demands it.

This is not "apply what you learned." This is "discover what you need." The sequence is reversed:

- Traditional: Teach → Practice → (Maybe) Apply
- This system: Encounter Problem → Discover Need → Learn Tool → Solve → Reflect

### 2. Full Coverage, Zero Gaps

Every 高考 knowledge point has a home in a problem set. This is non-negotiable.

Parents and students must never face the anxiety of "are we covering enough?" The mapping documents (`mapping/`) provide explicit correspondence between curriculum standards and PBL problems.

### 3. 主体性 as Architecture, Not Aspiration

Agency ("我可以...") is not a value we hope students absorb. It is a structural outcome of how learning is organized:

- Students choose which problems to pursue (within bounded sets)
- Students articulate their own hypotheses before receiving information
- Students present and defend their work to peers
- Students reflect on what they figured out, not what they were told

At every age, the question is: "What did YOU do?" — not "What were you taught?"

### 4. Developmental Honesty

A 3-year-old and a 17-year-old need radically different things:

- At 3: sensory richness, language immersion, safe exploration, no formal instruction
- At 10: structured projects with growing independence, cross-subject connections
- At 17: deep research, exam strategy integration, capstone synthesis

This system does not pretend one approach works for all ages. Each stage has its own logic while maintaining the through-line of agency and problem-driven learning.

### 5. AI as Amplifier

AI tools are introduced gradually and intentionally:

- **Ages 2-9**: AI is invisible (used by teachers/parents to design problems, not by students)
- **Ages 10-12**: AI as "research assistant" — students learn to ask good questions
- **Ages 13-15**: AI as "thinking partner" — students learn to critique AI output
- **Ages 16-18**: AI as "collaborator" — students learn to build with AI, knowing its limits

The goal: students who can use AI powerfully because they understand the problems deeply enough to direct it.

### 6. The 高考 Endgame is Honest

By ages 16-18, exam preparation intensifies in any system. This system's claim is:

- PBL foundations make exam prep **more effective** (deeper understanding → faster recall)
- Students approach exams with confidence ("I've solved harder problems than this")
- The transition from PBL to focused exam prep is a **gear shift**, not an identity crisis

We do not pretend 高考 doesn't matter. We prepare students to handle it from a position of strength.

## What This System Is NOT

- **Not "alternative education"** — It operates within 体制内, not outside it
- **Not anti-exam** — It is pro-understanding, which produces better exam results
- **Not laissez-faire** — Structure is carefully designed; students have freedom within scaffolding
- **Not tech-dependent** — AI enhances but is not required; the core is problem-driven learning
- **Not Western PBL copy-paste** — It is designed for the Chinese education context, timeline, and exam system

## The Measurable Claim

A student educated in this system should:

1. Score at or above peers on 高考 (same knowledge, deeper understanding)
2. Be able to articulate what they know and why it matters (主体性 evidence)
3. Use AI tools productively without dependency
4. Approach novel problems with "I can figure this out" rather than "I wasn't taught this"
5. Retain curiosity and motivation through age 18 — not despite the system, but because of it
