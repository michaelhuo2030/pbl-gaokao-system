# Stage 5: Architect (Ages 16-18, Grades 10-12 / 高中)

## Developmental Reality

Late adolescence. Students are capable of sophisticated abstract reasoning, long-term planning, and genuine intellectual contribution. Many are also facing intense external pressure: 高考 determines university placement, which in Chinese society carries enormous weight.

This is the critical test of the system: **can PBL foundations make 高考 preparation more effective AND more humane?**

## Core Mode: Capstone Research + Strategic Exam Synthesis

Two parallel tracks running simultaneously:

1. **Capstone Projects** — Deep, semester-long research producing real intellectual artifacts
2. **Exam Synthesis** — Systematic, strategic 高考 preparation that leverages PBL understanding

These are not in tension. The claim: students with deep understanding from years of PBL prepare for exams faster and perform better than students with shallow, drilled knowledge.

## What 高考 Requires

### Core Subjects (All Students)
- **语文**: Classical and modern Chinese literature, 文言文, essay writing (800+ words), poetry analysis, language usage
- **Math**: Functions, calculus basics, probability/statistics, analytic geometry, sequences, logic
- **English**: Reading comprehension, cloze tests, writing, grammar

### Elective Track (3+1+2 system in most provinces)
Students choose:
- **1 from**: Physics or History (determines science/humanities track)
- **2 from**: Chemistry, Biology, Politics, Geography

Total: 6 subjects examined. Score determines university admission.

## The Architecture: Two Tracks

### Track A: Capstone Projects (Grade 10 + Grade 11 first semester)

Semester-long research projects that represent the culmination of PBL education:

#### Example: "AI and Employment in Our City"

**Mission**: "Investigate how AI is changing employment in [local city]. Collect real data, analyze trends, project scenarios, and propose policy recommendations."

**Subjects integrated**:
- **Math**: Statistical analysis, function modeling, probability
- **语文**: Research report writing, interview transcription and analysis, persuasive policy writing
- **English**: Read international research papers on AI employment
- **Politics**: Labor law, economic policy, governance
- **Geography**: Regional economic differences
- **Technology**: Use AI tools for data analysis — while investigating AI's impact

**Depth**: Students produce a 5000+ word research report, present findings publicly, and submit to a real audience (local government, school board, media).

#### Example: "Designing a Sustainable Building"

- **Physics**: Structural mechanics, thermodynamics, electricity
- **Chemistry**: Material science, insulation chemistry
- **Math**: Optimization, geometry, cost modeling
- **Geography**: Climate considerations, site analysis
- **语文**: Technical writing, presentation
- **Art**: Architectural design, CAD basics

### Track B: Exam Synthesis (Grade 11 second semester + Grade 12)

Strategic, intensive 高考 preparation — but built on a foundation of deep understanding.

#### Why PBL Students Have an Advantage

| Exam Challenge | PBL Student Response | Traditional Student Response |
|---|---|---|
| Novel problem format | "I've solved unfamiliar problems before. Let me analyze what this is asking." | "I haven't seen this exact type. I don't know the formula." |
| 语文 essay (800 words) | Draws on years of project writing, genuine arguments, personal voice | Reproduces template essays with memorized examples |
| Physics multi-step problem | Thinks from first principles; has built things that use these concepts | Searches memory for which formula applies |
| Reading comprehension | Has read widely for projects; comprehends in context | Practices passage-type drills |

#### Exam Synthesis Method

1. **Curriculum Audit** (one-time, start of Grade 11 Semester 2): Map every 高考 knowledge point. Identify which ones were covered through PBL projects, which have gaps.

2. **Gap Sprint** (4-6 weeks): Intensive targeted work on any knowledge points not fully covered. For PBL students, this list is typically short — most content was encountered through projects.

3. **Format Training** (ongoing): Practice exam-specific formats — timing, question types, marking schemes. This is NOT about learning content; it's about translating existing knowledge into exam performance.

4. **Timed Practice** (weekly): Full-length or section-length practice under exam conditions. Review focuses on strategy, not content.

5. **Stress Inoculation**: PBL students are accustomed to ambiguity and unfamiliar problems. Exam stress is managed through this resilience, plus specific techniques.

## Journaling: The Architect's Reflective Practice

At this stage, journaling serves multiple functions:

### Weekly Reflection Prompts

1. **Sensing** (Presencing practice): "Where am I right now — intellectually, emotionally, motivationally? What am I noticing about my learning process?"
2. **Strategy**: "What's working in my preparation? What's not? What will I change?"
3. **Connection**: "How does what I'm studying connect to problems I actually care about?"
4. **Agency**: "This week I mastered... / I figured out how to... / I realized I can..."
5. **Future Self**: "What kind of person am I becoming through this work?"

### The 80/20 in Exam Season

Even during intensive exam prep:
- **80% of journal attention**: Maintaining motivation, managing stress, sustaining the "我可以" identity
- **20%**: Tracking knowledge gaps and study efficiency

The journal prevents exam preparation from becoming a dissociative, soul-crushing grind. Students stay connected to *why* they're doing this.

## 主体性 at Ages 16-18

Agency at this stage is existential:

- **"我选择..."** (I choose...) — active ownership of the exam process, not passive submission
- **"这对我重要因为..."** (This matters to me because...) — personal meaning, not just social pressure
- **"我知道我能，因为我已经..."** (I know I can, because I already have...) — confidence from real accomplishments

### Concrete Practices

1. **Exam strategy ownership**: Students analyze their own performance data and design their own study plans (with mentor support)
2. **Capstone defense**: Before exam season, students publicly present their capstone research — a concrete reminder of their capability
3. **University exploration as a project**: Researching universities and majors is itself an investigative project, not a passive process
4. **Peer study groups**: Student-organized, student-led. Not teacher-directed cram sessions.
5. **Post-高考 vision**: Students articulate what they want to do AFTER the exam — keeping the larger arc alive

## AI Integration

**AI as full collaborator — with sophisticated judgment:**

- AI-assisted research for capstone projects (with full transparency and source verification)
- AI as study partner for exam prep: explaining concepts, generating practice problems, identifying weak areas
- Students assess AI's strengths and limits in their subject areas
- **Capstone projects may investigate AI itself** — students become contributors to AI discourse, not just consumers
- **Meta-skill**: Students can articulate what AI can and cannot do in their field of study

## Assessment

### Capstone (Grade 10 + 11 first semester)
- Research quality and depth
- Intellectual rigor and originality
- Public defense
- Process documentation (journal)
- Real-world feedback from external audience

### 高考 Readiness (Grade 11 second semester + Grade 12)
- Monthly diagnostic exams (full format)
- Gap analysis → targeted intervention
- Performance trends tracked and student-owned

### The Final Measure

A student at age 18 should:
1. **Perform well on 高考** — the system's credibility depends on this
2. **Know who they are as a learner** — what interests them, how they learn, what they're capable of
3. **Have a body of real work** — capstone projects that demonstrate genuine capability
4. **Use AI as a powerful tool** — not dependent, not afraid, just competent
5. **Say "我可以" and have evidence** — 16 years of documented growth

## The Honest Risk

If PBL students score *lower* on 高考 than traditionally educated peers, this system fails — regardless of how much agency and motivation they have.

The bet: deep understanding + strategic exam preparation = same or better scores + a whole, motivated human being.

This bet is testable. The system should be evaluated rigorously against traditional approaches. See `risk/risk-analysis.md` for detailed risk analysis.

## What Success Looks Like at Age 18

- [ ] 高考 scores at or above cohort average
- [ ] A capstone project they're genuinely proud of
- [ ] Can articulate their intellectual identity and direction
- [ ] Uses AI productively in research and problem-solving
- [ ] Approaches university / career with confidence and curiosity, not burnout
- [ ] Says "高考 was hard but I was ready" — not "高考 was the worst experience of my life"
- [ ] Has a journal showing 6+ years of intellectual growth
- [ ] Knows what "我可以" means from lived experience
