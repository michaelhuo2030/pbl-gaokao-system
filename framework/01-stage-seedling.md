# Stage 1: Seedling (Ages 2-5)

## Developmental Reality

Children at this age learn through sensory experience, play, language immersion, and social interaction. Formal instruction is counterproductive. The brain is building foundational wiring for language, number sense, spatial reasoning, and social cognition.

## Core Mode: Guided Exploration Through Rich Environments

No worksheets. No "lessons." Instead: carefully designed environments and interactions that embed learning in play.

## What 体制内 Requires at This Stage

Kindergarten curriculum standards (《3-6岁儿童学习与发展指南》) cover:
- Health (身体, habits, safety)
- Language (listening, speaking, early literacy, early reading)
- Social development (interpersonal, social adaptation)
- Science (inquiry, math cognition)
- Art (appreciation, expression)

## How PBL Works at Age 2-5

"PBL" at this age means **structured play with intentional exposure**, not formal projects.

### Examples

| Curriculum Area | Traditional Approach | PBL Seedling Approach |
|---|---|---|
| Number sense (1-10) | Counting drills, worksheets | Cooking together: "We need 3 eggs. Can you get them? How many do we have now?" |
| Shape recognition | Flashcards | Building with blocks: "Can you make a tall tower? What shape is this piece?" |
| 语文 early literacy | Character flashcards | Storytelling circles, picture books with discussion, labeling things in the home |
| Science inquiry | "The sun is a star" lesson | "Why is there a shadow here? Let's go outside and look at shadows. Where does yours go?" |
| Social skills | "Share with friends" instructions | Group cooking project: "We're making 饺子 together. Who kneads? Who fills?" |

### Daily Structure (Sample)

1. **Morning circle** — "What do you notice today?" (observation practice)
2. **Exploration time** — Sensory stations, building, nature, art (self-directed with adult facilitation)
3. **Story / language time** — Read-aloud + discussion ("What do you think happens next?")
4. **Outdoor inquiry** — Nature walks with questions ("How many different leaves can you find?")
5. **Group project** — Cooking, building, gardening (collaborative, tangible outcome)
6. **Reflection** — "What did you figure out today?" (early 主体性 practice)

## 主体性 at Age 2-5

Agency at this age looks like:

- **"我可以自己..."** (I can do it myself) — dressing, pouring, choosing activities
- **"我发现了..."** (I discovered...) — noticing patterns, asking "why"
- **"我想试试..."** (I want to try...) — initiating exploration, not waiting for instructions

### Concrete Practices

1. **Choice boards**: 3-4 activity options; child chooses. Not "do this" — "which do you want?"
2. **Wait before helping**: Count to 10 internally before intervening. Let them struggle productively.
3. **Narrate their agency**: "You figured out how to stack those! You tried three ways and found one that works."
4. **"Show me" culture**: Instead of testing ("What color is this?"), invite ("Show me something blue!")
5. **Document their work**: Photos of their buildings, drawings, discoveries. Review together: "Look what you made."

## AI Integration at This Stage

**AI is invisible to the child.** It is a tool for parents and educators:

- AI generates age-appropriate problem prompts for adults to use
- AI helps parents understand developmental milestones
- AI can suggest responsive language based on what the child is exploring
- No screens for "learning apps" — the real world is the curriculum

## What Success Looks Like at Age 5

A child leaving the Seedling stage should:

- [ ] Count to 20+ and understand quantity (not just recitation)
- [ ] Recognize basic shapes and use spatial language naturally
- [ ] Have a large spoken vocabulary with emerging interest in characters/words
- [ ] Ask "why" and "how" questions spontaneously
- [ ] Be able to sustain focused exploration for 15-20 minutes
- [ ] Say "我可以" and mean it — attempt tasks independently before seeking help
- [ ] Collaborate with peers on simple group tasks
- [ ] Have no anxiety about "learning" — it's just what they do

## Alignment with Later Stages

Everything here is pre-academic foundation:

- Number sense → Mathematics
- Spatial reasoning → Geometry, Physics
- Story comprehension → 语文 reading comprehension
- "Why" questions → Scientific inquiry
- Collaborative projects → Group research (later stages)
- "我可以" identity → 主体性 across all subjects
